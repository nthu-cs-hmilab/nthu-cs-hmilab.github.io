About

Human-Centered Machine Intelligence Lab

The Human-Centered Machine Intelligence Lab was established on July 1, 2020 in the Department of Computer Science at National Tsing Hua University, Taiwan.

Our research spans neuroimaging, computational neuroscience, medical AI, and human-centered intelligent systems. We study how the human brain encodes information and how machine intelligence can be designed to better support people.

We aim to develop trustworthy, explainable, and practical AI technologies that augment human capability rather than replace it.

Research

Our Research Areas

We combine human-centered design, machine learning, and neuroscience to build reliable and impactful intelligent systems.

Can we capture a photo and obtain immediate diagnostic support from chest X-ray images?

Trustworthy AI for Medicine

We develop human-centered AI systems for healthcare using medical images, physiological signals, and electronic health records for clinical prediction, decision support, and interpretable analysis.

How does the human brain represent and recognize faces and other complex information?

Brain Encoding and Decoding

We investigate how the brain encodes information and apply machine learning methods to decode neural activity patterns from neuroimaging data.

Can intelligent systems help people learn and interact more effectively?

Human-Machine Interaction

We design interactive systems that improve user experience, learning efficiency, and decision making through adaptive interfaces and neural feedback.

How can computational methods reveal the organization of the human brain?

Computational Neuroscience

We develop statistical and computational approaches for neuroscience research, including brain modeling, representation analysis, and neurobiologically informed learning.
